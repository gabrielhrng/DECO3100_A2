Israel and Palestine's War for Independence



Upon its declaration of independence in 1947,the United Nations had recognised Israel as a democratic state and part of the international community. Ever since this historic event, the development of the nation in the 21st century has been met with hostility from neighbouring territories due to opposing political and religious views.

Following the fall of the Ottoman Empire in 1918, Great Britain liberated and took control of Palestine, the region including Jordan, Modern day Israel and Palestine. The declaration submitted by the British Foreign Secretary was approved by the League of Nations in 1922, in hopes of paving the road for an established Jewish Homeland in Palestine. However, this entailed bringing under the current arab population of Palestine under Jewish rule, a notion that the Palestinians greatly opposed to. Clashing ideologically, and ethnically, the Arab palestinians were unwilling to relinquish control of their homeland to the Jewish population and over the 21st century, wars are being continuously fought over the division of borders, and the ownership of the capital city: Jerusalem.

The city of Jerusalem contains multiple religious landmarks, including such as the Temple Mount, the holy sites al-Aqsa Mosque and more.

Current efforts focused in achieving a unanimous solution exists in two options: The "One-State" or "Two-State solution". The One-State solution, focuses on consolidating the states of Israel and Palestine together into a singular, secular, democratic state, where both Israeli Jews and Palestininan Arabs live as citizens with equal rights. The two-state solution is where Israelis and Palestinians live separately in two states; co-existing separately yet peacefully. This solution, allows Israel to retain a Jewish majority and the Palestinian state with a Muslim Arab majority.
